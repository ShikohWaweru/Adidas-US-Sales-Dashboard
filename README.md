
## Adidas US Sales Dashboard (2020–2021)

An interactive Excel sales dashboard built on ~9,650 transaction records from Adidas's US retail operations across 2020 and 2021.

- **Dashboard design** — Built a single-view interactive dashboard with dynamic filtering via Slicers

---

### What was built
- **Sales Regions sheet** — Pivot Table summarising Total Sales by all 50 US states, paired with a choropleth-style breakdown for geographic comparison
- **Sales Method sheet** — Aggregated revenue split across the three sales channels: In-store ($356.6M), Outlet ($295.6M), and Online ($247.7M)
- **Retailers sheet** — Revenue ranked by retailer: West Gear ($243M), Foot Locker ($220M), Sports Direct ($182M), Kohl's ($102M), Amazon ($77.7M), and Walmart ($74.6M)
- **Sales Trend sheet** — Monthly and annual revenue trend from Jan 2020 to Dec 2021, revealing a 294% year-over-year growth from $182M (2020) to $717.8M (2021)
- **Interactive Dashboard** — Consolidated visual summary with Slicers enabling filtering by retailer, region, product, and sales method

### Key insights
1. **Massive YoY growth** — Total sales surged from $182M in 2020 to $717.8M in 2021 (~294% increase), likely reflecting recovery from pandemic disruptions
2. **In-store dominates** — In-store sales account for ~39.6% of total revenue despite the rise of e-commerce, suggesting Adidas's retail partnerships remain its core channel
3. **West Gear is the top retailer** — At $243M, West Gear outperforms even Foot Locker ($220M), making it the most valuable retail partner
4. **California and Florida lead by state** — With $60.2M and $59.3M respectively, these two states alone account for ~13.3% of total national sales
5. **Men's Street Footwear is the highest-margin product** — Consistent 50% operating margin across records, compared to 25–35% for apparel lines
6. **Online channel has room to grow** — At $247.7M (27.5% of revenue), the online channel trails in-store by over $100M, signalling an opportunity to invest in digital sales
