## Adidas US Sales Performance Analysis (2020–2021)

A full sales performance analysis and interactive Excel dashboard built on 11,664 transaction records from Adidas's US retail operations across 2020 and 2021, covering five regions, six retail partners, three sales channels, and six product lines.

### What Was Built

- **Sales Regions sheet** — Pivot Table summarising Total Sales by all 50 US states, paired with a geographic breakdown by region
- **Sales Method sheet** — Aggregated revenue and margin split across the three sales channels: In-store ($866.4M), Outlet ($20.5M), and Online ($22.4M)
- **Retailers sheet** — Revenue and margin ranked by retailer: West Gear ($245.5M), Foot Locker ($222.8M), Sports Direct ($184.1M), Kohl's ($103.3M), Amazon ($78.8M), and Walmart ($74.7M)
- **Sales Trend sheet** — Monthly and annual revenue trend from Jan 2020 to Dec 2021, capturing ~294% year-over-year growth from $184.1M (2020) to $725.2M (2021)
- **Interactive Dashboard** — Consolidated visual summary with Slicers enabling filtering by Retailer, Region, Product, and Sales Method
- **Written Report** — Full analysis document with four embedded charts, supporting data tables, and seven prioritised recommendations organised across three action areas: channel and pricing, retailer and regional strategy, and planning and forecasting

### Key Insights

1. **Sales nearly quadrupled year-over-year** — Total sales rose from $184.1M in 2020 to $725.2M in 2021 (~294% increase), and overall operating margin across both years sits at roughly 37%
2. **Online is small but by far the most profitable channel** — Online generates only 2.5% of total sales ($22.4M) but runs a ~49% operating margin, well above in-store's 36.5% and outlet's 41.1% — a clear case of an under-scaled, high-margin channel
3. **In-store dominates volume but trails on margin** — At $866.4M, in-store accounts for ~95% of total revenue but runs the lowest margin of the three channels
4. **West Gear and Foot Locker lead on revenue** — West Gear ($245.5M) narrowly leads Foot Locker ($222.8M), though Sports Direct runs the best margin of the major retailers at 40.8%
5. **Walmart has the lowest retailer margin** — At 34.6%, Walmart trails all five other retail partners despite contributing a solid $74.7M in sales
6. **The West region leads on revenue but lags on margin** — $272.3M in sales but only a 33.2% margin, the lowest of any region; the South is the inverse, with lower revenue ($144.7M) but the best margin nationally at 42.3%
7. **Men's Street Footwear is the strongest product line** — Leading on both revenue ($211.2M) and margin (39.7%); Men's Athletic Footwear has solid revenue but the weakest margin of any line at 33.8%
8. **Sales are seasonal within the growth trend** — 2021 outperformed 2020 in every calendar month, with a June–August peak (topping out at $79.3M in July) and a March trough ($39.6M)
9. **Geographic concentration** — New York, California, and Florida are the top three states by sales, together accounting for ~20.4% of national revenue

### Recommendations Summary

| # | Action | Owner |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | Invest in growing the online channel — highest margin (~49%) but only 2.5% of sales | E-commerce/Digital |
| 2 | Review Men's Athletic Footwear's cost and pricing structure — lowest margin product line | Merchandising & Product Finance |
| 3 | Investigate Walmart's margin gap relative to other retail partners | Retailer/Channel Partnerships |
| 4 | Use the South region's cost structure as a benchmark for the West | Regional Sales & Finance |
| 5 | Study Sports Direct's margin performance for replicable practices | Retailer Partnerships |
| 6 | Build 2022 budgets and inventory plans around 2021's growth base and seasonal pattern | FP&A & Supply Chain |
| 7 | Decide whether to deepen investment in top states or grow underweighted markets | Regional Sales Leadership |
